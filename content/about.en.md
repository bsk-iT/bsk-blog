I'm Bruno Cubero, a technology enthusiast since childhood. My starting point was at my father's company: **Windows 98**, **Pentium III**… good times. I loved poking around the system. And, of course, playing the classics: **Pinball**, **Solitaire** and **Minesweeper** - who hasn't?

Since then, that passion has only grown. Technology, systems and hardware have always caught my attention. I'm naturally curious: whenever something sparks my interest, I go after it to learn and put it into practice. That's what drives me and, in the end, what makes me who I am.

I've been putting off creating this blog for a long time, it's actually a long-standing idea. The goal here is to have a place where I can document my ideas, projects and hobbies. Maybe it'll inspire someone (or not :D). But I also want to help anyone interested - friends, colleagues and anyone else - to understand, replicate and grow together. And it serves as an "information backup": when I need something, I can come back here and remember.

Think of it as a living repository. A technical diary. Just public. Anyway, here it is. I hope you enjoy it. Leave a comment. Exchanging ideas is always enriching.
